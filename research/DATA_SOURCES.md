# Comprehensive Dutch Genealogy Data Sources

Last updated: 2026-03-15

This document lists ALL known data sources for Dutch genealogy research beyond the
basics already in use (OpenArchieven, WieWasWie, FamilySearch, Gelders Archief,
Het Utrechts Archief, Alle Drenten, BHIC, MyHeritage).

## 0. MyHeritage (Read-Only Research Tool)

- **URL:** <https://www.myheritage.com>
- **Cost:** Free Basic plan available (tree cap at 2,500 people); paid plans for full access
- **Login:** Required (store credentials in `.env`)
- **Access:** Playwright browser automation only (no usable API)

### Key Features

- **Discovery Hub:** Record Matches and Smart Matches across your tree — birth/death dates, places, occupations, parents, siblings, children, photos.
- **SuperSearch:** 39 billion records (newspapers 19.4B, family trees 9.5B, BMD 4.7B, censuses 1.8B, etc.). Free plan shows summaries; some full records require Data subscription.
- **Smart Matches:** Automatic matching against other users' trees.
- **Connected family sites:** Access to other researchers' trees (some with 100K+ people).
- **Photo tools:** Colorize, enhance, animate old photos (limited free/day).

### Usage Notes

- Use for **research only** (read-only). Gramps Web (or your local GEDCOM) is source of truth.
- Match data = Tier C/D evidence — use to guide archive lookups, not as authoritative.

**Relevance:** VERY HIGH. Matches and SuperSearch are invaluable for cross-referencing and discovering leads.

Legend:

- **Free** = no cost, no login required
- **Free/Login** = free but requires account creation
- **Freemium** = basic free, premium features paid
- **Paid** = subscription or per-search fee required

## 1. National Archives and Databases

### Nationaal Archief (National Archives of the Netherlands)

- **URL:** <https://www.nationaalarchief.nl/>
- **Contains:** 125 km of documents, 300,000 maps/drawings, 15 million photos. Archives of the national government, VOC, WIC, province of Zuid-Holland. WWII records, Red Cross archive (1.5 million people), colonial records.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** National-level records; Zuid-Holland province (not directly relevant to your regions, but holds military records, WWII records, and Red Cross tracing files for all of NL).

### CBG Centrum voor Familiegeschiedenis (Centre for Family History)

- **URL:** <https://cbg.nl/>
- **Collections portal:** <https://cbgverzamelingen.nl/>
- **Contains:** Family dossiers with genealogical research results, the Central Archive of the Deceased, a genealogical library (searchable by name/subject/place), digitized Red Cross cards (10,000+ cards of Dutch WWII dead), family name database (320,000+ names).
- **Cost:** Free for online searches; some services require visit to The Hague
- **Login:** No for basic search
- **Relevance:** HIGH. The family dossiers may contain completed research on your surnames. The Red Cross cards are relevant for WWII-era family members. The Repertorium DTB (nationwide inventory of church records) is essential for pre-1811 research.

### CBG Familienamen (Family Names Database)

- **URL:** <https://www.cbgfamilienamen.nl/>
- **Contains:** 320,000+ Dutch family names with distribution maps (2007 and 1947 census), name explanations, historical mentions, literature references.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** HIGH. Look up de Knijf, van den Hul, Nijhof, Heezen, van Heukelom to see geographic distributions and name origins.

### StamboomNederland

- **URL:** <https://stamboomnederland.nl/>
- **Contains:** Uploaded GEDCOM files from researchers; surname information; a central repository of published Dutch family trees.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No for searching
- **Relevance:** MEDIUM. May contain trees overlapping with your surnames.

### Archieven.nl

- **URL:** <https://www.archieven.nl/>
- **Contains:** Centralized portal to finding aids (inventarissen), image banks, indexes of personal data, library catalogs from dozens of participating Dutch archives. NOT a record database itself but a gateway to archives.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** HIGH. Essential for locating specific record groups in any Dutch archive. Use it to find inventories for Barneveld, Ede, Putten, Nijkerk, etc.

### Delpher (Historical Newspapers, Books, Periodicals)

- **URL:** <https://www.delpher.nl/>
- **Contains:** 2 million+ digitized Dutch newspapers (1618-1995), journals, books, radio bulletins. Full-text OCR searchable. Run by the Koninklijke Bibliotheek (National Library).
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** VERY HIGH. Search for family names in newspaper announcements (births, marriages, deaths, obituaries), advertisements, news articles. Excellent for 1700s-1950s period. Search for "de Knijf" Barneveld, "van den Hul" Ede, etc.

### Geheugen van Nederland (Memory of the Netherlands)

- **URL:** <https://geheugen.delpher.nl/>
- **Contains:** Digital heritage collections from 100+ Dutch museums, archives, and libraries. Paintings, drawings, photographs, posters, newspaper clippings, stamps.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** LOW-MEDIUM. More cultural heritage than genealogy, but may contain photos or documents related to your regions.

## 2. Regional and Municipal Archives

### Gemeentearchief Barneveld (Municipal Archive Barneveld)

- **URL:** <https://gemeentearchief.barneveld.nl/>
- **Contains:** Civil registration, population registers (1850-1920), DTB church records (searchable via Gelders Archief website). Local history collections.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** VERY HIGH. Primary archive for de Knijf family research in Barneveld.

### Gemeentearchief Ede (Municipal Archive Ede/Bennekom)

- **URL:** <https://gemeentearchief.ede.nl/>
- **Contains:** 288,850+ records with 299,557 personal details. Archives for Ede, Bennekom, Lunteren, Otterlo, Wekerom, Harskamp, Ederveen. DTB records for Bennekom transferred here. Photos, maps, newspapers.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** VERY HIGH. Primary archive for van den Hul and other Bennekom/Ede families.

### Gemeentearchief Putten (Municipal Archive Putten)

- **URL:** <https://www.putten.nl/gemeentearchief/>
- **Contains:** Population registers (1850-1920), municipal government archives (1795-1939), transcribed DTB records (published by Vereniging Veluwse Geslachten), copies of Nijkerk housing data for Putten sections A-K. Razzia of Putten (October 1944) documentation.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** HIGH. Essential for Putten family research. Also contains Nijkerk cross-references.

### Erfgoedcentrum Zutphen (Heritage Centre Zutphen / Regional Archive)

- **URL:** <https://erfgoedcentrumzutphen.nl/>
- **Contains:** Archives for municipalities of Brummen, Lochem, and Zutphen. 815,339 historical documents with 1,137,561 personal references. Private archives of families, churches, businesses. Building permits, historical maps, image materials.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** HIGH for Zutphen family lines.

### Regionaal Historisch Centrum Rijnstreek en Lopikerwaard (Woerden)

- **URL:** <https://rhcrijnstreek.nl/>
- **Contains:** 1,100+ archives and collections covering Woerden, Bodegraven, Lopik, Montfoort, Oudewater, Reeuwijk, IJsselstein, and water boards. Municipal, business, institutional, and personal archives.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** VERY HIGH. Primary archive for the Woerden/van Heukelom line.

### Erfgoed 's-Hertogenbosch (Heritage 's-Hertogenbosch)

- **URL:** <https://www.erfgoedshertogenbosch.nl/>
- **Contains:** 900+ archives for 's-Hertogenbosch, Rosmalen, Bokhoven, Empel, Engelen, Nuland, Orthen, Vinkel. Population registers (1810-1939), housing registers (1930-1994), servant records (1860-1939), civil registration, DTB records, surety/discharge letters.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** VERY HIGH. Primary archive for 's-Hertogenbosch/Rosmalen families.

### Regionaal Archief Nijmegen

- **URL:** <https://regionaalarchiefnijmegen.nl/>
- **Contains:** 1,300+ archives for Nijmegen, Berg en Dal, Beuningen, Druten, Heumen, Lingewaard, Wijchen, etc. Population registers (1820-1920), 70,000+ records with 500,000+ person registrations. Digital reading room.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** MEDIUM. Relevant if any family lines pass through the Nijmegen area.

### Drents Archief (Drenthe Provincial Archive)

- **URL:** <https://www.drentsarchief.nl/>
- **Contains:** 6.7 million person records for Drenthe. Civil registration (1811-1974), DTB church records (1600-1811), population registers, notarial records (1810-1915), and uniquely the Maatschappij van Weldadigheid bedelaarskolonie (beggar colony) registers from Veenhuizen and Frederiksoord (1822-1866).
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Skill:** `.claude/skills/drents-archief.md` — Memorix Genealogy REST API
- **Relevance:** MEDIUM. Not in OpenArchieven. Relevant for Drenthe ancestors and bedelaarskolonie records (Christiaan Taks).

### Erfgoedcentrum Achterhoek en Liemers (ECAL)

- **URL:** <https://www.ecal.nu/>
- **Contains:** Archives for eastern Gelderland municipalities (Aalten, Berkelland, Bronckhorst, Doetinchem, Montferland, Oost Gelre, Oude IJsselstreek, Winterswijk). Archives, image collections, books, old newspapers. DTB/BS records flow through Gelders Archief to WieWasWie/OpenArchieven. Doesburg garrison records NOT digitized (physical visit only).
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** LOW-MEDIUM. Only relevant if family lines extend into eastern Gelderland (Achterhoek).

### Collectie Gelderland

- **URL:** <https://www.collectiegelderland.nl/>
- **Contains:** 750,000+ objects from Gelderland museums, archives, libraries, historical associations. Heritage objects, photographs, artworks.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** MEDIUM. Cultural heritage portal for Gelderland; good for contextual research and historical images.

### Mijn Gelderland

- **URL:** <https://mijngelderland.nl/>
- **Contains:** Historical stories, routes, organizations, events, and canons for Gelderland municipalities. Includes the Putten Razzia canon.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** MEDIUM. Background and context for Gelderland family history.

## 3. Military Records

### Nederlands Instituut voor Militaire Historie (NIMH)

- **URL:** <https://www.nimh.nl/>
- **Contains:** Military-historical collections from the Eighty Years' War to present. Personal files of the Royal Navy (Koninklijke Marine). Library with 70,000+ books. Growing online image database. 178,897 indexed records via OpenArchieven (Stamboeken, Persoonskaarten, POW files, decorations).
- **Cost:** Free (visit or email inquiry)
- **Login:** No
- **Skill:** `.claude/skills/nimh.md` — OpenArchieven API (`archive_code=nim`)
- **Relevance:** HIGH if any ancestors served in the military or navy. Contact: nimh@mindef.nl

### Militieregisters.nl (Militia Records)

- **URL:** Domain defunct. Data available via OpenArchieven.
- **Contains:** Conscription records for all Dutch males aged 18+ between ~1811-1940. Name, date/place of birth, occupation, parents' names, physical description, reason for exemption, regiment assigned. 2.4 million indexed records across 20 archives.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Skill:** `.claude/skills/militieregisters.md` — OpenArchieven API (`sourcetype=Militieregisters`)
- **Relevance:** VERY HIGH. All male ancestors in the 1811-1940 period should appear here. Excellent for finding parents' names and physical descriptions. Note: geographic gaps (no Zeeland, Groningen, Drenthe, Overijssel, Limburg).

### Nationaal Archief - Army Service Records

- **URL:** Via Nationaal Archief or FamilySearch
- **Contains:** Service records of non-commissioned officers and enlisted men, 1807-1929. Military career details, promotions, places served, pensions, conduct, birth info, physical description, parents' names.
- **Cost:** Free (on FamilySearch)
- **Login:** FamilySearch account (free)
- **Relevance:** HIGH for ancestors who actually served (rather than just being conscripted).

### VOC Opvarenden (Dutch East India Company Crew)

- **URL:** <https://service.archief.nl/hub3/api/nt/nt00444> (API)
- **Contains:** 853,785 crew records from VOC qualified muster rolls (1699-1794). Name, origin, rank, ship, fate (died/returned/deserted), service dates, VOC chamber, links to original scans.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Skill:** `.claude/skills/voc-opvarenden.md` — NA HUB3 JSON API
- **Relevance:** HIGH. Multiple Knijf/Knijff sailed as VOC soldiers/sailors. Daniel Pieterse Knijf from Woerden (1704). Note: `vocsite.nl` is ships only, `vocopvarenden.nationaalarchief.nl` is defunct.

### SSNE — Scotland's Soldiers and the Dutch Republic

- **URL:** <https://www.st-andrews.ac.uk/history/ssne/>
- **Contains:** Records of Scottish soldiers and families who served in the Dutch Republic's military (~1570-1782). Scots-Dutch Brigade records. ~5,000 entries.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Skill:** `.claude/skills/ssne.md` — URL-based search (no API, but curl-friendly)
- **Relevance:** HIGH for Jeths/Jets line. Captain John Henderson (SSNE 8038), Lt-Col James Balfour (SSNE 8009), Col David Balfour (SSNE 8033) all found here.

## 4. Church and Parish Records

### Van Papier naar Digitaal (VPND)

- **URL:** <https://www.vanpapiernaardotaal.nl/> (subsidiary of GeneaKnowhow)
- **Contains:** Images and indexes of church records (DTB: doop, trouw, begraven) and other pre-1811 sources including censuses, resident lists, and tax records. Transcriptions available.
- **Cost:** Freemium (some content requires subscription)
- **Login:** Required for some content
- **Relevance:** HIGH. Critical for pre-1811 research. May have Gelderland church records not yet on other platforms.

### GeneaKnowhow

- **URL:** <https://www.geneaknowhow.net/>
- **Contains:** Portal/directory connecting to thousands of links for genealogical resources per province and per town in the Benelux. Includes scanned historical records.
- **Cost:** Freemium
- **Login:** Some content restricted
- **Relevance:** HIGH. Use as a directory to find specific resources for Barneveld, Ede, Putten, Nijkerk, Woerden, etc.

### Repertorium DTB (CBG)

- **URL:** Available as PDF from CBG website
- **Contains:** Nationwide inventory of all existing Dutch church records (DTB) by Willem Wijnaendts van Resandt. Lists what records exist, for which period, and where they are held.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** VERY HIGH. Essential reference to know which church records exist for each municipality and where to find them.

## 5. Newspaper Archives

### Delpher Kranten (Newspapers)

- **URL:** <https://www.delpher.nl/nl/kranten>
- **Contains:** 2 million+ digitized newspapers, 1618-1995. Full OCR text search. Birth, marriage, death announcements; obituaries; advertisements; news articles.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** VERY HIGH. Already listed above but worth emphasizing for genealogy. Family announcements (familieberichten) are a goldmine.

### Regional Newspaper Archives

Various regional archives maintain their own newspaper collections:

- Gemeentearchief Ede has local Ede newspapers
- Erfgoedcentrum Achterhoek en Liemers has old Achterhoek newspapers
- Many municipal archives have newspaper clipping collections (knipselcollecties)

## 6. Cemetery and Burial Records

### Online Begraafplaatsen

- **URL:** <https://www.online-begraafplaatsen.nl/>
- **Contains:** 1.2 million+ persons across 1,700+ Dutch cemeteries. Updated daily by volunteers. Photos of gravestones where available.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** HIGH. Note: many Dutch graves are cleared after 20-30 years, so older ancestors may not be found. Jewish cemeteries are never cleared.

### Graftombe.nl

- **URL:** <https://www.graftombe.nl/>
- **Contains:** Volunteer-photographed and indexed gravestones from various Dutch cemeteries and graveyards.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** MEDIUM. Overlaps with Online Begraafplaatsen but may have different cemeteries covered.

### Het Stenen Archief (The Stone Archive)

- **URL:** <https://www.stenenarchief.nl/> and <https://eng.stenenarchief.nl/>
- **Contains:** Digitally mapped Jewish cemeteries in the Netherlands. 146 cemeteries completed. Photos, names, burial dates. Includes 28,000 records from Beth Haim (oldest Jewish cemetery in NL).
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** LOW (unless researching Jewish lines). Jewish cemeteries are never cleared, so records go back centuries.

### Oorlogsgravenstichting (War Graves Foundation)

- **URL:** <https://www.oorlogsgravenstichting.nl/>
- **Contains:** Registry of all war graves of Dutch citizens, whether in the Netherlands or abroad. 180,000 registered war victims. Searchable database.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** HIGH for WWII-era research, especially given the Putten/Arnhem connection.

### Find a Grave / BillionGraves

- **URLs:** <https://www.findagrave.com/> / <https://billiongraves.com/>
- **Contains:** International cemetery databases. Limited Dutch coverage compared to Dutch-specific sites.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No (Find a Grave); free account for BillionGraves
- **Relevance:** LOW. Online Begraafplaatsen is far more comprehensive for the Netherlands.

## 7. Notarial Records

Notarial records (notariele akten) contain wills, estate divisions, guardian appointments, land transfers, marriage contracts, and more. The notarial system was established in 1531.

### Where to Find Them

- **Regional/provincial archives:** Most notarial records are held at the provincial archive (e.g., Gelders Archief for Gelderland, RHC Rijnstreek for Woerden area).
- **Archieven.nl:** Search for notarial archive inventories.
- **FamilySearch:** Has microfilmed many Dutch notarial records, especially for Noord-Holland, Zuid-Holland, Utrecht, and Zeeland (pre-1811).
- **OpenArchieven:** Some indexed notarial records.
- **Erfgoed 's-Hertogenbosch:** For 's-Hertogenbosch/Rosmalen notarial records.

**Relevance:** VERY HIGH for pre-1811 research and for understanding property, inheritance, and family relationships.

## 8. Population Registers (Bevolkingsregisters)

Continuous population registration began January 1, 1850. Records list all occupants at each address with birth dates, occupations, family relations, and are updated with address changes, births, deaths, and marriages.

### Where to Find Them

- **Municipal archives:** Each municipality's archive holds their own registers (see Section 2 above).
- **OpenArchieven:** 32.1 million indexed population register records.
- **WieWasWie:** 26.7 million indexed records.
- **FamilySearch:** Collection spanning 1574-1940 (images, some indexed).
- **Ancestry:** Netherlands Population Registers Index, 1720-1945.

### Access Restrictions

Records are only public if the person has been deceased for approximately two years.

**Relevance:** VERY HIGH. These are the most comprehensive records for the 1850-1940 period.

## 9. DNA and Genetic Genealogy

### MyHeritage DNA

- **URL:** <https://www.myheritage.com/dna>
- **Contains:** DNA matching, ethnicity estimates. Most popular DNA testing company in the Netherlands.
- **Cost:** Paid (test kit ~EUR 49-79)
- **Login:** Required
- **Relevance:** HIGH. Best database for finding Dutch DNA matches. Since you already use MyHeritage, this is the natural DNA platform.

### AncestryDNA

- **URL:** <https://www.ancestry.com/dna/>
- **Contains:** Largest DNA database globally (25M+ tested). Strong North American coverage. Can upload results to MyHeritage and FamilyTreeDNA.
- **Cost:** Paid (test kit + subscription for full features)
- **Login:** Required
- **Relevance:** MEDIUM. Fewer Dutch testers, but the largest overall database.

### FamilyTreeDNA (FTDNA)

- **URL:** <https://www.familytreedna.com/>
- **Contains:** Y-DNA, mtDNA, and autosomal tests. Netherlands project group exists. First company to ship to NL at reasonable rates. Accepts uploads from other companies.
- **Cost:** Paid (test kits vary)
- **Login:** Required
- **Relevance:** MEDIUM-HIGH. The Vereniging Veluwse Geslachten has a FTDNA project group. Y-DNA testing could confirm patrilineal de Knijf connections.

### GEDmatch

- **URL:** <https://www.gedmatch.com/>
- **Contains:** Third-party comparison platform. Upload DNA from any testing company. Advanced tools for segment analysis, triangulation, ethnicity comparison.
- **Cost:** Free basic; GEDmatch Genesis paid
- **Login:** Required (free)
- **Relevance:** MEDIUM. Useful for comparing DNA across platforms.

### 23andMe

- **URL:** <https://www.23andme.com/>
- **Contains:** Ethnicity estimates, health reports, DNA matching. Does NOT accept uploads from other companies.
- **Cost:** Paid
- **Login:** Required
- **Relevance:** LOW-MEDIUM. Limited Dutch database. Privacy concerns for Dutch users.

- **Cost:** Free

- **Relevance:** MEDIUM. Only relevant if ancestors emigrated. Check for any family members who went to the Americas.

- **Cost:** Paid (Ancestry subscription)

- **Relevance:** Same content as Rotterdam Archives but with Ancestry search interface.

### CBG Emigration Resources

- **URL:** <https://cbg.nl/dutchancestors/emigration/>

- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No

## 11. Tax Records and Land Registry (Kadaster)

### Kadaster (Land Registry)

- **URL:** <https://www.kadaster.nl/>
- **App:** Kadasterviewer (for browsing digitized historical maps and registers)
- **Contains:** Land ownership records from 1832 to present. Records the owners of all plots and ships, rights attached to property, mortgages. If your ancestor owned property, they will be in the kadaster.
- **Cost:** Paid (per information request). Historical records (1832-1985) accessible via Kadaster employees. Post-1985 available online.
- **Login:** Required for online access
- **Relevance:** HIGH for property-owning ancestors (farmers, landowners). Barneveld/Veluwe families who owned farms will appear here.

### Historical Tax Records (Belastingkohieren)

Found in regional/municipal archives. Types include:

- **Verpondingskohieren** (property tax registers) - from late 1500s
- **Speciekohieren** (specific tax registers)
- **Collateral succession tax** (1591-1811) - tax on inheritances from non-direct relatives
- **Marriage and burial tax** (1695-1805) - covers ALL religions, valuable for non-Reformed families

**Where to find:** Municipal and regional archives (Gelders Archief, RHC Rijnstreek, etc.)

**Relevance:** HIGH for pre-1811 research. Tax records often predate church records and cover all religions.

## 12. Guild and Occupational Records

Guild archives (gildenarchieven) are held at municipal and regional archives. They typically contain:

- Membership lists
- Apprenticeship records
- Master craftsman registrations
- Financial records

**Where to find:** City archives of the relevant town. Erfgoed 's-Hertogenbosch for Den Bosch guilds. Erfgoedcentrum Zutphen for Zutphen guilds.

**Relevance:** MEDIUM. Depends on whether ancestors were guild members. More relevant for urban ancestors in 's-Hertogenbosch or Zutphen than rural Veluwe families.

## 13. Court Records and Criminal Records

### Court Records (Rechtbank)

Found at regional archives in the provincial capital. Types include:

- **Voluntary cases** (vrijwillige rechtspraak) - most genealogically useful. Property transfers, guardianship appointments, estate matters.
- **Criminal cases** (criminele rechtspraak) - interrogation protocols, witness statements, court minutes, verdicts.

### Prison Records

- **BHIC** has digitized all prison records for Brabant with an index of 100,000+ individuals.
- Other provincial archives hold their own prison records.
- The "inschrijvingsregister" (admittance register) is the most useful: physical description, crime, sentence, behavior.

**Where to find:** Provincial archives. Gelders Archief for Gelderland courts. BHIC for Brabant.

**Relevance:** MEDIUM. Check for any ancestors who had legal matters. Voluntary court records are useful for property and guardianship research.

## 14. Genealogy Communities and Forums

### Nederlandse Genealogische Vereniging (NGV)

- **URL:** <https://www.ngv.nl/>
- **Contains:** Largest Dutch genealogy association (8,200+ members). 27 regional departments. Library of 16,000+ titles in Bunnik. Journal "Gens Nostra" (6x/year). Regional meetings, lectures, courses.
- **Cost:** Membership required for full access (approximately EUR 40/year)
- **Login:** Required for members
- **Relevance:** HIGH. Join the Gelderland or Veluwe regional department for local contacts and specialized help.

### Vereniging Veluwse Geslachten (VVG)

- **URL:** <https://www.veluwsegeslachten.nl/>
- **Contains:** Genealogical research focused on Veluwe families. Database of ~2 million names from members. Library in Barneveld with 2000+ Veluwe books (many digitized). 400+ publications including DTB transcriptions for Veluwe towns, "De Herengoederen", "Kelnarij van Putten". Journal "Veluwse Geslachten" (4x/year). FTDNA DNA project.
- **Cost:** Membership required
- **Login:** Required for database access
- **Relevance:** EXTREMELY HIGH. This is THE specialized association for your core research area (Barneveld, Ede, Putten, Nijkerk, Apeldoorn). Their database of 2 million names from the Veluwe is invaluable. Their library in Barneveld likely has specific research on your families.

### Stamboom Forum

- **URL:** <https://www.stamboomforum.nl/>
- **Contains:** Active Dutch genealogy discussion forum. Ask questions, exchange tips, request lookups. Sister project of Genealogie Online.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** Required to post
- **Relevance:** HIGH. Post questions about specific surnames or brick walls.

### Dutch Genealogy (Yvette Hoitink)

- **URL:** <https://www.dutchgenealogy.nl/>
- **Contains:** Expert blog and resource hub for Dutch genealogy by certified genealogist Yvette Hoitink, CG. Tutorials, record type explanations, research tips, terminology guides. Professional research services available.
- **Cost:** Free (blog); paid for professional research
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** HIGH. Excellent reference for understanding Dutch records and methodology.

### Stamboomgids.nl

- **URL:** <https://www.stamboomgids.nl/>
- **Contains:** Comprehensive Dutch-language directory of genealogy resources: archives, family trees, surnames, websites. Organized by region.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** MEDIUM. Good directory for finding additional resources by region.

### Facebook: Dutch Genealogy Group

- **URL:** Search "Dutch Genealogy" on Facebook
- **Contains:** Community where people help each other with Dutch genealogy brick walls.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** Facebook account
- **Relevance:** MEDIUM. Good for getting help from other researchers.

## 15. Other Family Tree Platforms

### Genealogie Online

- **URL:** <https://www.genealogieonline.nl/>
- **Contains:** Online family trees published by Dutch genealogists. Many researchers prefer this over MyHeritage for sharing Dutch trees. Integrated with Stamboom Forum.
- **Cost:** Free basic; premium features paid
- **Login:** Required to publish; browsing mostly free
- **Relevance:** HIGH. Search for your surnames - other researchers may have published trees with overlapping family lines.

### Geneanet

- **URL:** <https://en.geneanet.org/>
- **Contains:** 9 billion individuals indexed. Family trees, photos, old books/newspapers. Strong European focus. Upload GEDCOM files.
- **Cost:** Freemium (premium for full access)
- **Login:** Required
- **Relevance:** MEDIUM-HIGH. Good European database; may have Dutch family trees not on other platforms.

### Geni.com

- **URL:** <https://www.geni.com/>
- **Contains:** Collaborative world family tree with 204 million+ connected profiles. Goal is one profile per person in the "Big Tree." Dutch language community exists.
- **Cost:** Free basic; Geni Pro paid
- **Login:** Required
- **Relevance:** MEDIUM. The collaborative approach means others may have already connected your family lines. Check the Dutch portal.

### Ancestry.com

- **URL:** <https://www.ancestry.com/>
- **Contains:** Major Dutch collections: Birth Index (1784-1923), Population Registers Index (1720-1945), Army Service Records (1807-1929), burial indexes, Genlias marriage/death indexes, GenealogieOnline tree index. Also has Holland America Line passenger lists.
- **Cost:** Paid subscription
- **Login:** Required
- **Relevance:** HIGH. Ancestry has imported many Dutch open datasets. Some collections are free.

## 17. WWII-Specific Records

### NIOD Institute for War, Holocaust and Genocide Studies

- **URL:** <https://www.niod.nl/>
- **Contains:** Archives, collections, and diaries related to WWII. 10.7% digitized. Research on occupation, resistance, persecution.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** HIGH for WWII family research.

### Oorlogsbronnen.nl (War Sources / Network of War Collections)

- **URL:** <https://www.oorlogsbronnen.nl/>
- **Contains:** Search engine aggregating 12 million+ sources from 250+ archives, museums, and libraries. Search for individuals. Photos, diaries, newspaper articles, films, letters.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** VERY HIGH. Central portal for all WWII-related Dutch records. Search for family members.

### Oorlogslevens.nl (War Lives)

- **URL:** <https://oorlogslevens.nl/> (via CBG/Oorlogsbronnen)
- **Contains:** 300,000 personal histories of WWII eyewitnesses. Timelines reconstructed from 1 million names across 20+ databases. Includes concentration camp prisoners, bombing victims, forced laborers, resistance fighters, soldiers, Jewish/Sinti/Roma victims.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** VERY HIGH. Search for family members who experienced WWII.

### Oorlog voor de Rechter (War in Court) / CABR

- **URL:** <https://oorlogvoorderechter.nl/> (from 2025)
- **Contains:** Central Archive on Special Jurisdiction (CABR): 4 km of files on 300,000+ suspected collaborators. Witness statements, NSB membership cards, diaries, pardon requests, photographs. Digitized with AI-assisted text recognition.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** MEDIUM-HIGH. Even if no family members were suspected, witness statements may mention your ancestors.

### Arolsen Archives (International Tracing Service)

- **URL:** <https://arolsen-archives.org/> / <https://collections.arolsen-archives.org/>
- **Contains:** World's most comprehensive archive on Nazi persecution victims. 50 million reference cards on 17.5 million people. Concentration camp records, forced labor documents, displaced persons files. 75-80% available online.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** HIGH. Especially for Putten razzia victims. The Stichting Oktober 44 found Putten deportee records here.

### Stichting Oktober 44 (Putten Razzia)

- **URL:** <https://www.oktober44.nl/>
- **Contains:** Database on the 589 men deported from Putten to Neuengamme concentration camp on October 1-2, 1944. Names, backgrounds, fates. Only 48 returned; 552 died.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** VERY HIGH given the Putten connection. If any de Knijf or related families were from Putten, check this database.

### Joods Monument (Jewish Monument)

- **URL:** <https://www.joodsmonument.nl/>
- **Contains:** Information on 104,000+ Jews who did not survive the Holocaust. Biographical details, last known addresses, deportation data.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** LOW (unless researching Jewish connections).

### Tweedewereldoorlog.nl

- **URL:** <https://www.tweedewereldoorlog.nl/>
- **Contains:** Thematic articles and documentation about WWII in the Netherlands. Organized by theme (resistance, persecution, reprisals including Putten).
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** MEDIUM. Background context for WWII events in your family's regions.

### Nationaal Comite 4 en 5 mei (War Memorials Database)

- **URL:** <https://www.4en5mei.nl/oorlogsmonumenten/>
- **Contains:** Searchable database of all war memorials and monuments in the Netherlands. Includes names inscribed on memorials.
- **Cost:** Free
- **Login:** No
- **Relevance:** MEDIUM. Check Putten, Barneveld, Ede memorials for family names.

## Summary: Choosing Priority Sources

Which sources matter most depends on your family's regions and time periods.
Use this general prioritization:

### Always Start With

1. **OpenArchieven / WieWasWie** — indexed civil registry records (1811+)
2. **Delpher** — newspaper search for surnames across all time periods
3. **Militieregisters.nl** — all male ancestors 1811-1940 (parents, physical descriptions)
4. **CBG Collections** — family dossiers, may have existing research on your surnames
5. **CBG Familienamen** — distribution maps showing where your surname originates
6. **Municipal archives** — primary sources for the specific towns your family lived in
7. **Archieven.nl** — gateway to find specific record groups in any Dutch archive

### WWII Research

8. **Oorlogsbronnen.nl** — central portal for all WWII-related Dutch records
9. **Oorlogslevens.nl** — 300K personal WWII histories
10. **Arolsen Archives** — Nazi persecution records (concentration camps, forced labor)
11. **Beeldbank WO2** — WWII photos
12. **Oorlogsgravenstichting** — war graves registry

### Extending Further Back (Pre-1811)

13. **Van Papier naar Digitaal / GeneaKnowhow** — church records (DTB)
14. **Repertorium DTB (CBG)** — inventory of all existing church records
15. **FamilySearch** — microfilmed Dutch records including notarial acts
16. **Regional archives** — notarial records, tax rolls, court proceedings

### Cross-Referencing

17. **Genealogie Online** — published Dutch family trees
18. **Ancestry.com** — Dutch collections (birth, population, army records)
19. **Geneanet / Geni.com** — European/global family trees
20. **Stamboom Forum** — ask questions, get help from other researchers
21. **Online Begraafplaatsen** — cemetery and gravestone records

### Specialized

22. **Kadaster** — land ownership records from 1832
23. **NIMH** — military service records
24. **MyHeritage DNA / FamilyTreeDNA** — DNA matching for breaking brick walls
25. **BYU Script Tutorial** — reading old Dutch handwriting
